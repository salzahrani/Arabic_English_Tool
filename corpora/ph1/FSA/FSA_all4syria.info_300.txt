Archived: The Syria they Wish for The One we will Create
– Posted on 2013/06/09 Posted in: Selected articles from Intl media
Mousab Alhamadee :all4syria
No day passes without the meeting of some of the world great powers’ representatives to discuss the future of Syria. Doing so, they are often negligent of the will of the Syrian people. Countries eager to achieve their interests in Syria are often unaware of the weight of the original agent who triggered the change that pushed diplomats to talk and delegations to meet. Some talk about the possibility that the country may fall apart; others express their worry (or wish) that Syria will follow the Lebanese example and indulge into years of civil war, while some imagine that only Islamic radicals will have the upper hand in Future Syria.
All parties are keen on containing the change in a way to achieve their own ends, and very few care about the number of deaths and the amount of destruction. On the other hand, Syrians pay no attention to all that buzz going on outside the borders of their country. They had goals in front of their eyes; on top of them come dignity and freedom. When they revolted to achieve these goals no one sent them an invitation to do that. And though they paid direly for their goals, they were aware of the high stakes from the beginning and they are ready (after the death of more than 100000 Syrian) to submit all the bill whatever high the price is. Syrians will not betray the blood of their martyrs by allowing Syria to become a ball on the playground of international players, nor they will accept falling under the rule of new dictatorship, or the reign of a clergy with a mentality belonging to the Middle Ages. 
Free Syrian Army (and other fighting groups) were able to liberate almost all the countryside of Syria and people living there possess what I call “Iron Free Will”. They had the option to leave to refugee camps in Jordan or Turkey (like what millions did), but they did not, expressing themselves in the process to all kinds of acts of brutal revenge from the side of the Syrian regime, starting from blocking civil services in their areas and ending with bombing them by military aeroplanes and scuds missiles.
These people are free now and they support the Free Syrian Army and other militants on the ground to liberate the big cities in the country and help the drink of honor and dignity they have just tasted reach to their fellowmen in occupied Syria.
If you are a news editor in a European newspaper, or an employee in the State Department, you can talk a lot about dividing Syria, the potentials of sectarian war, or even the possibility of the Assad regime taking over again. But if you, by any means, find yourself in direct contact with citizens of Free Syria, whether on the ground, or elsewhere in this world, you will find yourself ashamed of that bag of bad thoughts on the back of your mind. Their will must astound you, their sacrifice will undermine your interests and their determination will push you give your calculations another thought.
As a citizen of Free Syria I was all the time aware of the difficulties that might come in the way of my freedom. I can even say that I have always been alert to the dangers posed on our revolution, whether they came from chemical weapons, extremism, sectarian tensions, separatist tendencies, or even the international conflict over Syria that might really disrupt our interests. Understanding the features of that complex environment enveloping the Syrian issue is a vocal step towards developing the revolutionary strategy that will take our move to the desired end.
If we are to go into details in a more concrete language I might raise here the issue of arming Free Syrian Army, for example. Many of the international players think that without outside support the Free Syrian Army will not be able to win; consequently, they can always use this as a means to influence the situation and promote their own agenda whether they were allies or enemies to the Free Syrian Army and the public opposition. On the other hand, many ignore, or might not know, that the majority of arms in the hands of  the Free Syrian Army are spoils they obtained from military bases after they had attacked sites belonging to the regime army. So I find it redundant to say that what used to happen for two years now might happen in the future and on larger scales. I remember myself once saying to an American diplomat: though we are not getting arms from our American friends, we are getting them from our Russian enemies by taking, from time to time, new military bases  belonging to the Assad army.
As for those who have worry/wish to see sectarian war in Syria, I would like to ask them why haven’t we witnessed such a thing until now? Yes, I can agree that there is a sectarian war from one side. It is from the side of the sectarian Assad regime against the Sunni majority of the country. But that doesn’t happen in the opposite direction, which is – I think – a great moral victory to the opposition. Units from the Free Syrian Army have always had access to territories inhabited by Shabiha advocates, i.e. towns and villages from which Shabiha militants are recruited. What these units could have done – and they had never did – is to attack Alawite towns and villages every time the regime targets Sunni civilians. That would have applied automatic pressure on the regime to stop targeting the public cradle of the Free Syrian Army. As long as the Free Syrian Army is fighting for justice, and as long as this army is keen on protecting all Syrians; whatever their sect or ethnicity is, sectarian war will not erupt in Syria. Those who are worried: relax; those who wish for that: don’t ever dream of it.
Friends and enemies of Syria alike can have their own wishes for a Syria that can go along with their interests. But Syrian people are now taking their country out of the lap of dictatorship in order to create democratic, pluralistic and unified Syria in which all sects and ethnicities live side by side in dignity, freedom and prosperity. Creating that kind of Syria will automatically define those parties who will have interests to share with the Syrian people.
